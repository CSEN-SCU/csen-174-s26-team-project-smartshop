## Problem Framing Canvas Key Insights

**Date:** April 12, 2026

### The Problem
Budget-conscious shoppers have trouble figuring out where to buy the exact items they need at the lowest overall cost because comparing prices across multiple nearby stores is manual and time-consuming, and it's hard to judge whether a lower price is worth an extra trip.

### Why It Hasn't Been Solved
It's low priority: many shoppers tolerate workarounds (checking store apps one-by-one or defaulting to one store), and businesses haven't prioritized making cross-store comparison easy.

### Key Assumptions & Biases to Watch
- Designing for a "default" shopper risks missing edge cases — particularly shoppers with dietary restrictions or allergies, where "equivalent" items may not meet their needs.

- ### Who Experiences the Problem
- - **Who:** Shoppers trying to stay within a budget
  - - **When:** During grocery trips / when planning at home
    - - **Where:** Usually at home (planning/comparing)
      - - **Consequences:** They may overspend or forgo items
       
        - ### Who's Been Left Out
        - - Shoppers with dietary restrictions/allergies (risk that "similar" items don't meet their needs)
          - - People without cars / limited mobility (fewer feasible store options)
           
            - ### Reframed Problem Statement
            - Budget-conscious shoppers struggle to stay within their budget because prices vary across stores and it takes time and effort to compare options (including the travel tradeoff), which can lead to overspending or not buying everything they need.
